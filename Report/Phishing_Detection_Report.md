# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness Report

## 1. Introduction

Phishing is a cyberattack technique used by attackers to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, and personal information. This project analyzes phishing email samples and identifies indicators that can help users recognize and avoid phishing attacks.

## 2. Objective

* Analyze phishing email samples
* Identify phishing indicators
* Classify suspicious emails
* Provide cybersecurity awareness guidance
* Recommend preventive measures

## 3. Tools Used

* GitHub
* Public Phishing Email Datasets
* Email Header Analysis
* Browser Tools

## 5. Common Phishing Indicators Identified

* Different sender and reply-to addresses
* Failed SPF authentication
* Suspicious domains
* Encoded or misleading subject lines
* Impersonation of trusted brands
* Urgent security-related messages

## 6. Prevention & Awareness Guidelines

### Do's

* Verify sender email addresses
* Check domains carefully
* Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Report suspicious emails
* Verify requests through official channels

### Don'ts

* Do not click unknown links
* Do not download suspicious attachments
* Do not share passwords or OTPs
* Do not trust urgent messages blindly

## 7. Conclusion

The analyzed emails contained multiple phishing indicators including spoofed sender identities, failed authentication checks, suspicious domains, and deceptive messaging techniques. Users should remain cautious and follow cybersecurity best practices to avoid becoming victims of phishing attacks.
